What is the molar concentration?
The molar concentration is a measurement of the concentration of a chemical species, in particular of a solute in a solution, in terms of the amount of substance per unit volume of solution. The molar concentration is measured by mol/L.
How to calculate molar concentration?
To calculate molar concentration you should use the following formula:
M = n/v
where:
M = molar concentration
n = moles of solute
v = liters of solution
Why use molar concentration?
The molar concentration is used in order to convert between the volume of the solution and the moles of the solute.
